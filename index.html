The TLR4 signaling pathway is activated upon binding of lipopolysaccharide (LPS) to the TLR4/MD-2 complex. This interaction triggers the recruitment of adaptor proteins MyD88 and TIRAP to the intracellular domain of TLR4. MyD88 then recruits and activates IRAK kinases, leading to the formation of a signaling complex. This complex activates TRAF6, which subsequently activates the TAK1 kinase complex. TAK1 then phosphorylates and activates IKK and MAPK pathways. The IKK complex phosphorylates IκB, leading to its degradation and the release of NF-κB. Activated NF-κB translocates to the nucleus and induces the expression of pro-inflammatory genes. Simultaneously, the MAPK pathway activates AP-1 transcription factors, further amplifying the inflammatory response. This cascade results in the production of pro-inflammatory cytokines, chemokines, and other inflammatory mediators.
